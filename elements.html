Physical Features

The average elevation of Bangalore is 900m above sea level. Bangalore gets almost 970ml of annual rainfall, which is one of the highest for major cities in India. This rainfall is possible due to a phenomenon called the Palakkad gap. Bangalore is situated in Dharwar craton which is one of the oldest. There is a unique feature in Bangalore which is that the craton or the bedrock of the earth rises above the surface to form mountain ranges or hills, for example: Nandi hills. Savandurga in Bangalore is the largest rock in asia. In historical times, the east coast of India was sticking to Antarctica while the west coast was attached to Africa. The southern parts of India were sticking to Madagascar and when they split there was some kind of fault line or breakage in the Western Ghats, and this is called the Palakkad gap. This gap allows the rain clouds to break through the hill range of Western Ghats and precipitate in several regions across southern India. The most important feature of Bangalore is the high rainfall and its altitude. The topography of Bangalore is undulating with a central ridge running NNE-SSW. The highest point is Doddabettahalli, which is 962 m (3,156 ft) and lies on this ridge. No major rivers run through the city, though the Arkavathi and [Dakshin Pinakini river] cross paths at the Nandi Hills, 60 km (37 mi.) to the north.

Prominent Dynasties

In 14 th century, the Vijayanagara dynasty rose as a powerful one. Most of the temples which are existing today like Kanchi, are depicted by vijayanagara dynasty. They had a specific and iconic architecture shows Hindu god idles. Sanctum Santorini of Hoga Nandeshwar temple has a pillar with parrots belongs to 16th-17th century. They have very good administrative structure of ruling. Vijayanagars ruled through palakadu/ poligars. They were warriors and had taxation rights. They are also called nayakas. The Empire formed in 1334, collapsed in 1550 by the alliance of 5 sultanites. During the decline nayakas became independent. After the decline of the Empire, it was taken over by the sultanate of Bijapur or the Adil Shahi Dynasty.
